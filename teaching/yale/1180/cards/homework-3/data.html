9. Let $Y(K, L)$ be a function describing the economic output of a factory (i.e. the dollar value of everything it produces in a year), given the value $K$ of all its equipment and the number of person-hours $L$ of labor over the course of a year. The Cobb-Douglas model for this function describes it as $Y(K, L) = aK^bL^c$ for constants $a$, $b$, and $c$. If $a = 100$, $b = 0.25$, and $c = 0.75$, and $K$ and $L$ are measured in thousands, find $Y_K(200, 10)$ and $Y_L(200, 10)$ and interpret both of them. What does this say about the factory?
