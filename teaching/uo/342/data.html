12. Let $A$ be an $n \times n$ matrix with eigenvectors $\vec{v_1}, ..., \vec{v_n}$ and corresponding eigenvalues $\lambda_1, ..., \lambda_n$. If $A$ is invertible, what are the eigenvectors and eigenvalues of $A^{-1}$? Justify your answer.

13. Let $A$ be an $n \times n$ matrix with eigenvalues $\lambda_1, ..., \lambda_n$. What are the eigenvalues of $A^T$? Justify your answer.

What will the homework be like?

The homework is available on this site, and there will be roughly 20 problems due each week. Your grade on each is out of 20 points — 8 are for thoroughly attempting every problem regardless of whether your answers are correct, and I’ll select three problems per homework to grade on correctness for 4 points each.

All of our homework is submitted through Canvas. One option is to handwrite your work and then scan it with a scanning app that produces PDFs (e.g. the scanner in the Files app on iOS or Adobe Scan on Android). Another option that I highly recommend is to try typing your homework with Latex. This is how scientific papers and books (and our notes and homework) are written, and it has the benefit of being easily editable if you make a mistake part of the way through a problem. The simplest way to get started is to make an account at Overleaf, and to make things as easy as possible, there’s an option to download every homework as a Latex file. Typing your homework is completely optional, but I encourage you to give it a shot! If you plan to go into any STEM field, this is something you’ll have to learn eventually, and it’s a lot easier than you might expect.

As a high-level math class in the 21st century, I expect you to be able to make use of technology, but in a judicious and responsible manner. Unlike the quizzes and exams, which are closed to all resources except your handwritten notes, it’s a good idea to make use of all the resources available on the homework — but in the right order. To this end, please follow these rough steps when you’re working on a homework problem:

Start by working on it with no outside resources other than your handwritten notes. If you get stuck on a step for 10–15 minutes, look at the online notes. If you’re still stuck after another 5–10 minutes, then ask someone else in the class (either a friend or me). If you’ve exhausted all those options and aren’t able to make progress, looking up similar problems online can be helpful, but carries the potential risks of finding incorrect information and/or undermining learning. If you decide to consult the internet, make sure you’re aware of your sources and their reliability!

What are the skills I can expect to learn from this course?

We will learn how to:
