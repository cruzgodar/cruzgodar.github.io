The Hopf Fibration

The 3-sphere, written $S^3$, is the set of points in 4-dimensional space with distance 1 from the origin, just like the usual 2-sphere $S^2$ in 3-dimensional space. An incredible property of this higher dimensional sphere is that it’s expressible as a fibration over the 2-sphere: we can exactly fill up all of $S^3$ by attaching a circle (an $S^1$) to every point in $S^2$. My Thurston Geometry slides include a visualization of the fibration within $S^3$, but this applet renders them in euclidean space via stereographic projection. By using a variant of an exponential map instead of this projection, we can also compress the fibers into the unit ball, viewing the fibers from a more global perspective.

Drag on the scene to look around. On a keyboard, use WASD to move, and on a touchscreen, hold with two fingers to move forward and three to move back.
