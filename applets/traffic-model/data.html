The Biham-Middleton-Levine Traffic Model

After taking a few months away from applet development to rewrite my raymarching engine and rendering library, I wanted to return with something light. This model simulates a large city full of cars by rendering each as a pixel that moves one step either north (purple) or east (blue) every turn, so long as there is no other car in its way. For car densities below about 30%, the two groups largely can pass by one another uninterrupted, while densities larger than about 35% typically form large jams that take over the entire city. In between, more interesting behavior occurs, often with repeating patterns. It’s a great example of how extremely simple rules can give rise to complicated and beautiful behavior!
